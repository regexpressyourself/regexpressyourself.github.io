21.3 Unique assets to the program

While a two-year college starts students on a paralegal professional path, Southern Illinois University at Carbondale takes the career path to the next level, offering an American Bar Association-approved bachelor degree paralegal studies program. Your bachelor degree offers a choice of two paths, either pursuing a career as a professional paralegal or choosing instead our pre-law specialization to go on to law school.

Our pre-law specialization is particularly helpful in preparing for law school. We provide excellent law school preparation because we cover from a practical perspective many of the same courses a law student will be taking the student’s first few years in law school.

As a result, our graduates who go on to law school consistently report they have an advantage over other law students who came to law school from other undergraduate majors. We get tremendous feedback from our graduates’ experiences in law school. In today’s competitive legal market, every edge is a blessing.

Our general paralegal path is superior in that our students are specifically trained for practical performance in a professional setting immediately upon graduation. In your final semester, you will work in an internship for a law office. When it comes time to seek employment upon graduation, students with a four-year bachelor degree in paralegal studies have an edge over those who have completed two-year degrees; more-so, if that is a bachelor degree from an ABA-approved program.

At SIU Carbondale, Paralegal Studies is the only undergraduate program where every teacher is a licensed attorney. Among other requirements, Paralegal students must take Introduction to Law, two Legal Writing and Research classes, Civil Procedure, Trusts and Estates, Business Entities, Family Law, Torts, Bankruptcy and Creditors’ Rights, and Technologies in the Law. Criminal law, criminal procedure, contracts, and property law are also popular courses offered to our paralegal students.

Students who wish to move out of the area from where their associate degrees were earned will improve their competitive edge in the field by obtaining their ABA-approved paralegal Bachelor degree from SIU Carbondale.
